# 7.1 Introduction to Geospatial Health Data

Location plays a crucial role in public health decisions, from tracking disease outbreaks to determining where to establish new health facilities.

- The field of epidemiology heavily relies on geospatial data to track and predict disease spread. Through this data, researchers can identify hotspots and work proactively to contain outbreaks.
- By mapping out healthcare facilities, authorities can ensure equitable access to care and identify underserved areas that may need more support.
